Technology Development in Progress

The Technology

Genesis Pulse is developing a new class of solid-state pulsed power technology capable of delivering high-energy pulses with improved reliability and operational stability.

The architecture is designed to address key challenges associated with high-fluence, repetitive pulsing, including thermal and mechanical stress management at the material interfaces.

Current Status

  • Provisional patent filed with the USPTO
  • Early-stage proof-of-concept development underway
  • Seeking technical collaboration and manufacturing partners

Potential Applications

The technology is being developed with several high-value applications in mind:

  • Aerospace & Space Systems — Compact, high-performance pulsed power for electric and pulsed plasma propulsion, power conditioning, and onboard systems requiring high reliability in demanding environments.
  • Advanced Manufacturing — High-energy pulsed processes such as magnetic pulse welding and electromagnetic forming, particularly for dissimilar metals and high-performance structures.
  • Scientific & Research Facilities — Reliable, repeatable pulsed power delivery for laboratories, high-energy physics experiments, and specialized diagnostic systems.

Core Design Approach

Click below for a high-level overview of the design philosophy.

The architecture employs a symmetric, multilayer solid-state design that incorporates specialized interfacial layers. These layers are intended to maintain material stability and performance under repeated high-energy pulsing conditions.

This approach focuses on managing internal stresses and preserving interface integrity during demanding operational cycles.
